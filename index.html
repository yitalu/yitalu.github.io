I am a PhD candidate in Political Science and a graduate affiliate of the Center for Behavioral Political Economy at Stony Brook University.

My research applies computational and experimental methods (agent-based modeling, behavioral economic games) to study behavioral political economy. Substantive topics include collective actions, electoral competition, political polarization, and the spread of misinformation.

My dissertation concerns the collective action problems in interest group coalitions and how the might of policy opponents affects their cooperative behavior. The three chapters consist of a game-theoretic model, an agent-based simulation, and a lab experiment. The study aims to provide insight into climate negotiation, interest group politics, and human cooperation in general.
