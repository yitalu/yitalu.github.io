I am a political economist/scientist in the Department of Human Behavior, Ecology and Culture and the BirthRites Lise Meitner research group at the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology.

My research investigates cultural and demographic determinants and consequences of global development. Specific topics include how culture and value changes shape our modern world, the effects of population changes on politics, and the cultural evolution of fertility transition.

Broadly, I am interested in political economy â€” how political and economic factors interact and affect our society. I study cooperation in multilevel and political settings, particularly the collective action problem within a group when the members face the same out-group competitors. I am also interested in the relationship between political polarization and wealth inequality in the US context and the dynamics of electoral competition.

My research perspective incorporates political economy, behavioral economics, evolutionary anthropology, and cultural evolution. The methods include agent-based modeling, game theory, statistical inference, and lab experiments.

I have a background in political science, economics, and physics and received my Ph.D. in Political Science from Stony Brook University in May 2021.
